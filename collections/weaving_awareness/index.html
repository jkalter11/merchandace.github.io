Weaving Awareness

Sara Moodie is an autistic, asexual and agender adult living in Ontario, Canada. She writes: "In order to keep my mind focused, I need to be doing something with my hands. I found that making things was a perfect way to do that, and the things I made attracted quite a lot of highly positive attention from people who saw them. I decided to begin selling them online in the later part of 2015.

"Because of my own differences, the idea of supporting and honouring the things that make various people unique really appeals to me. I'm also asexual and agender, both of which fall under the umbrella terms of Queer, QUILTBAG, LGBT+, and MOGAII, so many of my designs reflect my support of everyone who identifies as belonging to the same groups. Many of my designs are inspired by awareness flags and logos used by these groups.

"Because so many of us feel invisible due to lack of representation and even outright erasure, I believe it is vital to take pride in your own uniqueness with a tangible sign that others can see (when it is safe for you to do so). To that end, many of the bag tags, key chains, bracelets, and other items for sale here contain Pride symbolism."
